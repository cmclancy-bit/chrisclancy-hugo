## Use Shortcuts and Apple Intelligence to take, access and organize your screenshots like a pro

Don't let your screenshots pile up on the Desktop or languish in some random folder with nothing more than a timestamp to distinguish them from one another. With Shortcuts and on device AI you can have your Mac handle your screenshots in the most intelligent way possible - organized and easily accessible form anywhere.

---

## Clutter abounds. And there's no good reason for it.

I take hundreds of screenshots every day at work, and until recently those files littered my desktop creating visual and mental clutter. It was hard to justify spending the time to clean them up and often I'd just resort to deleting everything and then realize later on that it actually would have been valuable to have a reference of some of the work I'd done earlier in the week that I'd failed to create a proper version of (I'll be going much more in depth about versioning in another post). 

The point is all of this isn't necessary. Your computer was meant to take care of this on you behalf. One of the most interesting things about AI, not to get too far off track, is that it's really raised by expectations about what a computer can (and should) do to help you get your work done. I've spent so much of my career working on computers that nowadays seem "compromised" for lack of a better word, that I've come to think of these kinds of inconveniences as just something to accept. No more!

## This is how it's supposed to work

So, unless you've been living under a rock (or you're a Windows or Linux user) you probably already know that the Mac has a number of ways you can automate a task. It's just that until very recently it required a lot of trial and error for less technical folks. Applescript, Automator, and shell scripting has been around for decades, but I believe the advent of Shortcuts and AI (literally everywhere) has sparked a completely new way of looking at and leveraging these tools.

Now that I'm completely off track it's probably a good time to explain how you can *actually* solve the problem of managing your screenshots. So without further ado...
