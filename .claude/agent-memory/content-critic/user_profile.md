Chris Clancy runs a personal website at chrisclancy.com focused on content design, UX writing, Figma, and the craft of clear communication. He is a practitioner — someone who ships real work — not a thought leader or consultant-brand type.

He has self-awareness about his own voice and branding: he flagged his own tagline as "self-aggrandizing and cringe," which suggests he values honesty and specificity over polish, and that he'll respond well to direct, unsentimental feedback.

Tone he responds to: confident, peer-level, no hand-holding. He wants the uncomfortable truth, not reassurance.

Audience for his site: likely other practitioners (UX writers, content designers, product designers) and hiring managers or collaborators in the product/tech space.

His site is built in Hugo and hosted at chrisclancy.com.

He is also writing tech/culture pieces aimed at curious non-experts — readers interested in home labs, self-hosting, and AI tools but without deep technical backgrounds. This is a different register than his UX writing content: more conversational, more "friend explaining something cool" than practitioner-to-practitioner. His natural voice in this mode is warm and enthusiastic but tends toward over-explanation and throat-clearing in early drafts. He is aware pieces can run long and wants help knowing when to cut.
